Different type of Penalities

Readmission Penalty

A Readmission is defined as a measure of patients who initially went into the hospital for one of five conditions but returned within 30 days of discharge. CMS penalizes hospitals for readmissions related to heart failure, heart attack, pneumonia, chronic lung problems, such as emphysema and bronchitis, and elective hip and knee replacements.

HCAHPS (Hospital Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems)

The HCAHPS survey is a standardized, publicly reported survey of patient's perspectives of hospital care. Patients rate their inpatient stay in 27 categories, ranging from communication with doctors and nurses to pain management to facility cleanliness and quietness. Based in part on these scores, hospitals can either lose or gain up to 2% of their Medicare reimbursement dollars.

HAC (Hospital Acquired Conditions)

HAC are defined as conditions that patients acquire while receiving treatment for another conditions in an acute in an acute care health setting. The HAC payment penalty is calculated as an adjustment of calculated Value Based Purchasing and Readmission Reduction programs. Payment adjustment would impact hospitals that rank among the lowest performing 25% with regard to HACs.
